Super Fun Chart Makes Boring Stuff Fun

Background

I'm a parent, and I use charts with stickers to motivate my five-year-old son, Charlie. Actually, he's five and a half.

Anyhow, the sticker charts work OK, but I often lose the piece of paper or the stickers. So I'm building Super Fun Chart as a way to make a little bit more engaging chart experience.

How to use Super Fun Chart

Use it to reward your children for positive behavior, like:

  • Staying in their room until the morning
  • Getting up and dressed in the morning on their own
  • Eating a good dinner

If your child just needs to eat four more bites, make a chart with four stars, and after each bite, let your child push the button to mark the progress.
